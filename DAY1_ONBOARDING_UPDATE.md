## Content Principles Applied

### 1. **Emotional Anchoring**
- Coach Prompt asks "why" before "how much"
- Reflection card requires written answer
- Acknowledges that "goals without emotional connection fade fast"

### 2. **Micro-Moments of Reflection**
- 3 distinct pause points (Coach Prompt, Check-In, Reflection)
- Each creates a moment to think, not just click through
- Buttons allow quick engagement without typing fatigue

### 3. **Permission to Start Small**
- "You can always start smaller and raise your goal later"
- "Adjust until it *feels* realistic"
- "Too high / Just right / Too low" acknowledges uncertainty

### 4. **Coaching Voice**
- Uses "we'll" instead of "you'll" (collaborative)
- "Most agents overestimate..." (normalizes struggle)
- "You've set your annual goal... That's more than most agents ever do" (validation)

### 5. **Progressive Disclosure**
- Card 1: Why + What
- Card 2: How (with calibration)
- Card 3: Connection (actions â†’ results)
- Card 4: Commitment (emotional anchor)

## Behavioral Design Rationale

### Why Ask "Why" First?
Research shows goals tied to intrinsic motivation (family, freedom) persist longer than extrinsic goals (money alone). By asking "why do you want to grow" before "how much," we anchor the goal emotionally.

### Why "Just Right" Sentiment Check?
Agents often set goals based on peer pressure or industry benchmarks. The check-in validates their internal compass and gives permission to adjust without shame.

### Why Written Reflection at End?
Writing creates commitment. When agents type "I want to give my kids a better life," they've created a psychological contract stronger than clicking "next."

### Why "That's More Than Most Agents Ever Do"?
Validation prevents drop-off. Many agents feel behind their peers. Acknowledging their progress builds momentum to continue Day 2.
